Triangle Centers

Drag the vertices to explore the properties of triangle centers.

Intersection of the medians (lines from vertices to opposite side midpoints). Always inside the triangle. Divides each median in a 2:1 ratio.

Intersection of the perpendicular bisectors. Equidistant from all vertices, making it the center of the circumcircle.

Intersection of the altitudes (perpendicular lines from vertices to opposite sides). Can fall outside the triangle.

Intersection of the angle bisectors. Equidistant from all sides, making it the center of the incircle.

A line that always passes through the Orthocenter, Centroid, and Circumcenter in any non-equilateral triangle.
